## Interview 1: The Enterprise Engineering Lead

* **Role:** Senior Software Engineer, Wipro
* **Team Type:** Internal engineering and development team

### Transcript Excerpt

> **Q: What was your first impression after testing OptiAI?**
>
> *Engineer:* "The idea is genuinely useful. Teams often keep paying for tools that developers stop using after switching workflows. I tested the site and the optimization breakdown felt practical enough that I could actually see a team using this internally."
>
> **Q: What improvements would make it stronger?**
>
> *Engineer:* "The UI already looks polished, but deeper optimization logic would make the recommendations feel more enterprise-ready. Tool overlap detection and usage-based suggestions could become a strong differentiator."

### Product Takeaways

* Real-world validation that engineering teams would actively use a tool like OptiAI.
* Stronger optimization algorithms and smarter overlap analysis can significantly improve enterprise credibility.
* Reinforced the importance of operational clarity and trustworthy recommendations.

## Interview 2: The Real-Time Systems Perspective

* **Role:** Software Engineer, TCS
* **Team Type:** Enterprise engineering workflows

### Transcript Excerpt

> **Q: What do you think could become the biggest future use case for OptiAI?**
>
> *Engineer:* "Manual inputs are fine for early-stage teams, but real-time API integrations would make this significantly more powerful. If the platform could directly analyze live AI usage patterns and token consumption trends, the optimization recommendations would become much more accurate."

### Product Takeaways

* Real-time AI provider integrations could become the platform’s strongest long-term differentiator.
* Usage-based optimization is more scalable and accurate than purely manual estimation flows.
* Future roadmap should include API-level tracking and trend intelligence dashboards.
